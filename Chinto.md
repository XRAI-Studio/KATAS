# Chinto – Isshin Ryu

- Rank level / when introduced: Intermediate kata (often after Wansu)
- Lineage / dojo notes: One‑leg emphasis, angular footwork; associated with fighting on a slope
- Starting direction: 45° to the left from front
- Key themes:
  - Diagonal embusen (fighting off the center line)
  - Strong use of cat and Chinto stance with one‑leg loading
  - Angled entries, leg checks, and arm control
- Performance notes:
  - Snappy transitions with clear directional changes
  - Emphasis on balance and precision, especially in one‑leg work

## Key principles

- Structure / shape:
  - Primarily built on a diagonal line with multiple returns to T‑stance and angular entries. [web:88][web:89]
- Main tactical ideas:
  - Off‑line angling, arm control/armbars, leg checks, and close‑range finishes. [web:87][web:90]
- Power / body mechanics:
  - One‑leg loading in Chinto stance, hip rotation in hammerfists/punches, and strong drop into low blocks. [web:86][web:93]
- Breathing:
  - Natural breathing with sharper exhale on hammerfists, dumps, and final finishing sequences.
- Stance cues:
  - Keep weight balanced on the diagonal, maintain strong cat/T‑stances for angle changes, and don’t let the one‑leg work collapse. [web:88][web:93]

## Common errors

- Treating Chinto stance as just “weird front stance” instead of a deliberate one‑legged, angled stance.
- Letting arms do all the work on reinforced blocks without hip/torso driving the lock/throw.
- Rushing the jump/X‑block sequence and losing structure and landing control.
- Letting T‑stances drift off the embusen instead of cleanly angling and re‑centering.

## Favorite applications (examples)

1. Early X‑guard → open‑hand block → hammerfist‑into‑palm:
   - Parry/cover high line, crash in, strike arm or head, then seize arm for lock or throw.
2. Jump front kick → X‑block:
   - Defense vs punch then kick, followed by block and check against low kick or tackle entry.
3. Hair‑grab and smash to leg:
   - Block punch, strike limb, seize head, drag down onto thigh/knee as a takedown.

## Training drills

- Solo:
  - Diagonal Chinto stances with open‑hand block → hammerfist sequences.
- Partner:
  - Armbar and shoulder‑lock drills built off the reinforced‑block positions.
- Pad:
  - Hammerfist and elbow combinations from Chinto stance into heavy bag or pad.
- Flow:
  - “Slope” sparring concept: only allow diagonal movement and T‑stance angle changes, using Chinto’s ideas.
